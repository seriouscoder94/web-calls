Initial Contact

1. Introduction Script

When they answer:

"Hi, is this [Name]? I'm [Your Name], and I noticed your [business type] while searching online. Do you have a moment to chat about increasing your online visibility?"

If they say yes:

"Great! I noticed that [Business Name] doesn't have a website yet. In today's digital age, you might be missing out on potential customers who are searching for [their service] online. Would you be interested in learning how a website could help grow your business?"

If they seem hesitant about cost:

"I understand the concern about cost. Did you know that 78% of local searches on phones lead to in-store visits within 24 hours? A website is like having a 24/7 salesperson working for you."

2. Value Proposition Script

Main Benefits:

"Let me share three ways a website could help [Business Name]: 1. 24/7 Visibility: Customers can find your information anytime 2. Credibility: Show reviews, photos, and your expertise 3. More Customers: Show up when people search for [service] in [city]"

Industry-Specific Benefits:

• "Restaurants: Accept orders without paying delivery app fees" • "Services: Let customers book appointments while you sleep" • "Retail: Show your products to people before they visit" • "Professional: Share your expertise and build trust"

3. Handling Common Objections

"I'm too busy"

"That's exactly why you need a website - it works for you 24/7 while you focus on running your business. We handle everything, so you don't have to worry about the technical details."

"I get enough business through word-of-mouth"

"Word-of-mouth is great! A website actually amplifies that. When people recommend you, the first thing new customers do is look you up online. Without a website, you might be losing those referrals."

"It's too expensive"

"Think of it as an investment. If a website brings you just one new customer per week, how much additional revenue would that generate? Most of our clients see positive ROI within the first few months."

"I'm not tech-savvy"

"No problem at all! We handle everything technical. You just provide the information about your business, and we take care of the rest."

4. Closing the Conversation

For Interested Prospects:

"Great! The next step would be a 15-minute video call where I can show you examples of websites we've created for other [business type] businesses. Would Tuesday or Wednesday at 2 PM work better for you?"

For Hesitant Prospects:

"I understand you need time to think about it. Would it be helpful if I sent you some information about how similar businesses are using their websites to grow? I can also include some specific ideas for [their business]."

For Not Interested:

"No problem at all. If you don't mind, I'll send you some information about digital marketing for [their industry]. Feel free to reach out if anything changes!"

Best Practices:

  • Listen more than you talk
  • Use their business name often
  • Focus on benefits, not features
  • Share relevant statistics
  • Respect their time
  • Always follow up with an email
